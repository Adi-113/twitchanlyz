Twitch Streamers Network Analysis

About the project

This network graph visualizes the connections between Twitch streamers based on shared games or collaborations. Each node represents a streamer, and links indicate shared top-streamed games. Use this visualization to explore community clusters and discover influential streamers within the network.

Which day of week is "Prime Time" for follower growth?

According to these Charts,While Tuesday is the most popular day among streamers, it does not correspond to the highest average follower gain, suggesting saturation or competition. Conversely, Sunday appears underutilized relative to its high follower gain potential, indicating an opportunity for increased visibility and growth. Streamers looking to maximize reach might consider shifting part of their schedule to Sundays and Fridays for higher impact.

How diverse is the platform linguistically?

The chart highlights a highly centralized linguistic landscape, with nearly half of all streamers using a single dominant language,English(48%). Other widely used languages include Japanese (11%), French (8.5%), Portuguese (7.5%), and German (5.5%), reflecting strong regional communities. This distribution suggests that while English-speaking streamers benefit from broader audience access and platform visibility, there is meaningful potential for growth among underrepresented language groups.

what communities naturally emerge Streamers' Gaming preferences?

We applied a community detection algorithm, Louvain method—to the network of streamers.The Louvain algorithm clusters streamers into modular communities that are more densely connected internally than with the rest of the graph. Each detected community was then labeled using the most commonly streamed game among its members, such as League of Legends, VALORANT, or Minecraft.

How does influence travel across the Twitch streamers' universe ?

The network visualization reveals three critical paths that define how influence flows across the Twitch streamer ecosystem. The longest path , connecting paulinholokobr from the GTA V cluster to tolkin from the League of Legends cluster, represents the most distant bridge—highlighting a potential bottleneck where content or trends take the most steps to traverse between communities. In contrast, the shortest path between pobelter and xarola_ links the LoL and VALORANT communities directly,acting as a fast lane for audience engagement. Most notably, the cross-community path weaves through key streamers spanning all six major game clusters, serving as the backbone of the network. These streamers are strategically positioned to influence multiple communities simultaneously and play a central role in unifying the fragmented streaming landscape.

Is the streamers' Network Small-World in Nature?

The Streamers' Graph is a small-world network.It has ahigh clustering coefficient (0.7241) , similar to a regular lattice, indicating strong local connectivity. At the same time, its small diameter (5) is closer to that of a random graph, enabling efficient global reach. This combination confirms that the network is small-world in nature.
